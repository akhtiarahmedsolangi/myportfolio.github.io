Epidural Technique

Epidural anesthesia is a medical technique used to provide pain relief during labor, childbirth, and certain types of surgery. Here are the general steps involved in using the epidural technique: Positioning: The patient is usually placed in a seated position or lying on her side with her back arched forward. Cleaning: The area where the epidural will be inserted is cleaned and disinfected with an antiseptic solution. Numbing: A small injection of local anesthetic is given to numb the skin and the deeper tissues. Insertion: Using a thin needle, the anesthesiologist inserts a small catheter into the epidural space, which is the area around the spinal cord and the nerves that transmit pain signals. The needle is carefully guided using fluoroscopy or ultrasound to ensure accuracy. Testing: Once the catheter is in place, a test dose of medication is injected to make sure that the epidural is working properly and that there are no complications. Dosing: Depending on the procedure, the anesthesiologist will determine the appropriate dose of medication to administer through the catheter. This is usually a combination of a local anesthetic and a narcotic pain medication. Monitoring: The patient's vital signs, such as heart rate, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation, are closely monitored throughout the procedure
